When Companies Bring Me In

Most companies already have capable engineers.

But as a company grows there are moments when delivery slows, confidence in the architecture starts to fade, or important technical decisions become harder to make.

These situations usually appear as the engineering team and systems enter unfamiliar territory — scaling pressure, architectural complexity, leadership gaps, or rising reliability and regulatory expectations.

At that point, adding more engineers rarely solves the underlying problem.

What leadership often needs instead is experienced technical judgement: someone who can step back, understand how the systems and team actually operate, identify where risk or complexity is building, and help decide what truly needs to change.

That is where I usually get involved.

Common Situations

  • Delivery used to feel predictable, but progress has become slower or inconsistent.
  • The system works, but confidence in the architecture is beginning to fade.
  • Engineers are working hard, yet progress feels harder than it should be.
  • The team is capable but lacks senior technical leadership for difficult decisions.
  • Founders want an experienced second opinion before making major technical bets.
  • Reliability, security, or compliance suddenly carry greater weight.
  • The business is growing faster than the technical foundations underneath it.

In these moments the issue is rarely a shortage of engineers.

What is missing is clear technical judgement at leadership level — someone who can step back, see the system as a whole, and help leadership decide what to change and what to leave alone.

Who I Work With

I typically work with organisations that already have a capable engineering team and established product, but are entering a phase where technical decisions carry greater consequences.

This usually includes:

  • Founders or leadership teams seeking experienced technical judgement as their company grows
  • CTOs or senior engineers who want a second opinion on difficult architectural or organisational decisions
  • Investors or boards looking for an independent view of technical risk or delivery confidence

In most cases the organisation is technically capable — the leadership team simply wants clearer visibility, stronger direction, or reassurance before making important decisions.

Many conversations begin simply as a second opinion on a situation that feels unclear.

What I Do

I help organisations reduce technical risk before it becomes organisational risk.

Organisations usually work with me in one of three ways.

Technical Diagnostic

A structured assessment of architecture, delivery, quality, and technical decision‑making.

This produces a clear view of how the system and engineering organisation are actually functioning, where risk is building, and which changes will have the greatest impact.

The goal is not documentation for its own sake, but practical clarity that leadership can act on immediately.

Focused Intervention

Where specific problems need resolving, I work directly with the team to stabilise delivery, architecture, or decision‑making structures.

This might include:

  • untangling architectural complexity
  • establishing clearer engineering leadership
  • improving delivery reliability
  • addressing security or compliance pressures

The aim is to restore momentum and confidence while leaving the organisation stronger than before.

Ongoing Advisory

Some organisations benefit from having experienced technical judgement available on an ongoing basis.

In these cases I act as a fractional CTO or technical advisor, helping leadership teams make better decisions as the company continues to grow.

Experience

Most recently I served as CTO of an EdTech company, guiding the engineering team and technical platform through a period of organisational change while strengthening delivery, reliability, and operational maturity.

Across these roles I have worked with mission‑critical systems used by organisations supporting vulnerable people, and implemented governance frameworks including GDPR, ISO 27001, ISO 9001, and ISO 22301 to ensure systems, teams, and processes remain dependable as organisations grow.

How I Work

My approach is calm and pragmatic.

I begin by understanding how the system actually behaves, how decisions are made, and where the real constraints lie. From there we identify the small number of changes that will make the greatest difference.

My perspective is grounded in many years building and operating systems myself, which allows me to move comfortably between architectural thinking, engineering detail, and leadership decisions.

The aim is not to impose process or theory, but to restore clarity and direction so that teams can move forward with confidence.
